what can you do

Golf clubs & resorts

Swin a club at 19th-century clubs or modern resorts' challenging golf courses.

Local wildlife

Visit protected nature reserves to search for elusive leopards, rare sloth bears & tropical birds.

Sunset cruises

Hop on a sailboat at a palm-studded sandy beach to watch the sun slip down into the Indian Ocean.

String hopper breakfast

Start the day with thin rice noodles doused with fiery sambal sauce, served in homey cafes.

Trishaw ride

Flag down a brightly colored motorized scooters to trundle around towns or along scenic rural roads.

Beachside beer

Order a cold lager at relaxed beachfront bars or upscale rooftop lounges overlooking the sea.

Waterside seafood dining

Dine on flavorful squid curries & chili crab at informal eateries dotting the waterfront.

Stilt fishermen

Spot fishermen casting their lines at sunset while they perch on poles in shallow waters.

Provinces

Brief History of Districts and province in Sri Lanka.

In 1833 a legislative council was created, making the island a politically and administratively single unit. Five provinces were created, later expanded into nine, and these were subdivided into twenty-one districts. These districts were administered by officials known as Government Agents or Assistant Government Agents. In 1955, the district replaced the province as the country's main administrative unit. Ampara District was created in April 1961, followed by the creation of Mullaitivu and Gampaha districts in September 1978 through a new constitution, which also reintroduced the province as the main administrative unit. The last district to be created was Kilinochchi in February 1984, and the current constitution (that of 1978) states that the territory of Sri Lanka consists of 25 administrative districts. These districts may be subdivided or amalgamated by a resolution of the Parliament of Sri Lanka. Since 1987 the 25 districts are purely administrative under the 9 Provincial Councils created in by the 13th Amendment to the Sri Lanka constitution. Note that although the Northern Province has Five Administrative Districts, it has only two Electorial Districts. Therefore there are only 22 Electorial Districts in Sri Lanka. Jaffna and Kilinochchi form the Jaffna Electorial District. Mannar, Vavuniya, and Mullaitivu form the Vanni Electorial District. The Ampara District is known as the Digamadulla Electorial District.
